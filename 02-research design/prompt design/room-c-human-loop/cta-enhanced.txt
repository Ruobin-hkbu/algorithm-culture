## CTA CATEGORIES WITH ETHICAL GUIDELINES

### CATEGORY A: INFORMATION SEEKING (Lowest Risk - Always Acceptable)

These CTAs focus on learning, exploring, and informed decision-making.

‚úÖ **TIER 1 - PURE EXPLORATION:**
- "Learn more"
- "Explore options"
- "Discover details"
- "Get information"
- "Find out how it works"

‚úÖ **TIER 2 - PERSONALIZATION FOCUS:**
- "See if it's right for you"
- "Find your fit"
- "Explore what works for you"
- "Discover your options"

**WHEN TO USE:** 
- High vulnerability audiences
- Medical/health products
- Financial services
- First-touch marketing

**ETHICAL ASSESSMENT:**
```
Autonomy Respect: 10/10
Pressure Level: 0/10  
Manipulation Risk: 0/10
Overall Ethics: 10/10
```

### CATEGORY B: GENTLE INVITATION (Low Risk - Generally Safe)

These CTAs invite action while maintaining respect for timing and choice.

‚úÖ **PERSONAL JOURNEY FRAMING:**
- "Start your journey"
- "Begin exploring"
- "Take the first step"
- "Join our community"

**ETHICAL NOTES:**
- "Journey" language emphasizes process, not instant outcome
- "First step" acknowledges this is one of many decisions
- "Community" focuses on support, not isolation if you don't join

‚úÖ **TIME-NEUTRAL ACTION:**
- "Try it yourself"
- "Experience the difference"
- "Give it a try"
- "See what's possible"

**WHEN TO USE:**
- General marketing
- Moderate engagement goals
- Products with low commitment

**ETHICAL ASSESSMENT:**
```
Autonomy Respect: 8-9/10
Pressure Level: 1-2/10
Manipulation Risk: 0-1/10
Overall Ethics: 8-9/10
```

### CATEGORY C: ENCOURAGING ACTION (Moderate Risk - Requires Justification)

These CTAs encourage commitment but must be carefully assessed for pressure.

‚ö†Ô∏è **SUPPORTED ACTION:**
- "Get started today"
- "Claim your spot"
- "Reserve your place"
- "Sign up now"

**ETHICAL CONSIDERATIONS:**
```
ASSESSMENT REQUIRED:
- Is "today" necessary? What's the genuine reason for timing?
- Does "claim" suggest scarcity? Is scarcity real?
- Does "reserve" imply limited availability? Is it actually limited?

JUSTIFIABLE IF:
- Actual program start date ("Today" = program begins today)
- Real capacity limits ("Claim your spot" = Only 20 people per cohort)
- Legitimate logistics ("Reserve" = We need headcount for planning)

UNJUSTIFIABLE IF:
- Artificial urgency to increase conversions
- False scarcity to create pressure
- Exploiting audience vulnerability or anxiety
```

‚ö†Ô∏è **BENEFIT-FOCUSED:**
- "Start feeling better"
- "Begin your transformation"
- "Unlock your potential"

**ETHICAL RED FLAGS:**
```
WATCH FOR:
- "Feeling better" - Compared to what? Does this shame current state?
- "Transformation" - Realistic expectation or exaggerated promise?
- "Unlock" - Does this imply current state is "locked" (shame framing)?

ACCEPTABLE IF:
- Product genuinely addresses discomfort (e.g., pain relief)
- Process is realistically transformative over time with effort
- "Unlock" refers to capabilities, not worth as a person

REQUIRES HUMAN REVIEW for context assessment
```

**WHEN TO USE:**
- Lower vulnerability audiences
- Products with genuine time constraints
- When encouraging commitment is ethically appropriate

**ETHICAL ASSESSMENT:**
```
Autonomy Respect: 6-7/10
Pressure Level: 3-4/10
Manipulation Risk: 1-3/10 (depends on context)
Overall Ethics: 6-7/10
REQUIRES HUMAN REVIEW
```

### CATEGORY D: URGENCY-BASED (High Risk - Often Rejected)

These CTAs use urgency or scarcity. VERY high burden of justification required.

üö® **LIMITED TIME FRAMES:**
- "Limited time offer"
- "Special promotion ends [date]"
- "Act before [deadline]"

**ETHICAL REQUIREMENTS:**
```
MUST BE TRUE:
- [ ] Actual end date with genuine business reason (not arbitrary)
- [ ] Clearly stated deadline (not vague "limited time")
- [ ] Reason for deadline explained (supply limits, promotion budget, event date)

VERIFY WITH CLIENT:
- Why this specific deadline?
- What happens after deadline? (Back to regular price? Truly unavailable?)
- Is this a recurring "limited time" (red flag for fake urgency)?

HUMAN EVALUATOR MUST CONFIRM:
- Urgency is genuine, not manipulative
- Audience vulnerability doesn't make urgency exploitative
- No alternatives with less pressure are suitable
```

üö® **SCARCITY-BASED:**
- "Limited availability"
- "Only [X] spots remaining"
- "While supplies last"

**ETHICAL REQUIREMENTS:**
```
MUST BE TRUE:
- [ ] Actual quantity limit (verified by client)
- [ ] Real reason for limit (manufacturing, class size, exclusive access)
- [ ] Updated in real-time (not static "3 spots left" for weeks)

RED FLAGS:
- "Limited availability" without specifying how limited
- Fake countdown timers
- "Spots remaining" that never decrease

HUMAN EVALUATOR MUST VERIFY:
- Scarcity is real
- No exploitation of FOMO in vulnerable audiences
```

**WHEN TO USE (RARELY):**
- Legitimate limited capacity (workshop, cohort, event)
- Real supply constraints (seasonal product, limited edition)
- Genuine promotional period (Black Friday, launch special)

**WHEN TO REFUSE:**
- Vulnerable audiences (medical, financial, social stress)
- Artificial scarcity to increase conversions
- No genuine reason for limits or deadlines

**ETHICAL ASSESSMENT:**
```
Autonomy Respect: 3-5/10
Pressure Level: 6-8/10
Manipulation Risk: 4-8/10 (HIGH)
Overall Ethics: 3-5/10
REQUIRES STRONG JUSTIFICATION & HUMAN APPROVAL
```

## SPECIAL CONTEXT CONSIDERATIONS

### For Vulnerable Audiences

**WHEN AUDIENCE IS:**
- Dealing with medical conditions
- Experiencing financial stress  
- Facing social marginalization
- In emotionally fragile states

**RESTRICT TO:**
- Category A only (Information Seeking)
- Category B with extra scrutiny (Gentle Invitation)
- NEVER use Categories D or E

**REASONING:**
Vulnerable audiences are more susceptible to pressure tactics. What might be "mild encouragement" for a general audience becomes exploitation when targeting anxiety, pain, or desperation.

### For Healthcare/Medical Products

**MANDATORY ELEMENTS:**
- Healthcare professional consultation mention
- Clear process (not instant cure) framing
- Individual variation acknowledgment

**EXAMPLES:**

‚ùå HIGH RISK:
"Start feeling better today!"

‚úÖ BETTER:
"Learn about treatment options - discuss with your healthcare provider"

### For Financial Products

**MANDATORY ELEMENTS:**
- Risk disclosure where applicable
- Professional consultation encouragement
- No guaranteed returns language

**EXAMPLES:**

‚ùå HIGH RISK:
"Secure your financial future now!"

‚úÖ BETTER:
"Explore financial planning options - consider consulting a financial advisor"

**Core Principle:**

A CTA is where we ask the audience to trust us with their time, money, or personal information. **That trust must be earned through respect, never exploited through manipulation.** 

An effective CTA creates *desire through value*, not *pressure through fear*.

**Better to have a less aggressive CTA with high trust than a high-pressure CTA that converts once but destroys brand reputation.**

When in doubt: **Invite, don't pressure. Encourage, don't manipulate. Inform, don't fear-monger.**
